about American Eskimo

The American Eskimo Dog is a breed of dog that originated in Germany.

Its name, this breed has no connection to the Eskimo culture or way of life.

The American Eskimo Dogs, also known as the Eskie, come in three sizes—standard, miniature, and toy. They have double coats which are dense and white. And their coats are used to keeping out the cold.

They have Nordic type ... with erect triangular shaped ears, and they have distinctive black points on their faces such as their lips, noses, and eye rims. They also have plumed tails carried over the back. American Eskimo Dogs are highly intelligent, playful, and affectionate. Eskies are very social and thrive on human companionship. They make excellent family pets and are good with children, as long as they are properly socialized and trained from a young age.

history of American Eskimo

Early 1800s

In the early 19 century, the successive waves of German immigrants that reached American shores beginning in the early 1800s had a profound impact on the development of the Midwest.

The German immigrants brought American Eskimo Dogs with them and they were known as German Spitz at that time.

German Spitz were used as farm dogs by German immigrants in the U.S. These little white dogs were ancestors of the modern Eskie.

Late 1800s

Because of the intelligence, agility and showy appearance, the German Spitz became mainstays of trained-dog acts in the circuses until the twenty century.

Early 1900s

Upon America entered into World War I in 1917, The breed’s German name was changed to “American Eskimo” Dog due to anti-German sentiment during World War I.

Late 1900s

The American Eskimo Dog Club of America was founded to promote and preserve the breed. The American Kennel Club officially recognized the American Eskimo Dog as a breed.

Present

the American Eskimo Dog is a beloved family pet and companion known for its loyalty, intelligence, and affectionate nature. It continues to be a popular breed in the United States and around the world.

how to take best care of your Eskies

health tips

It's important to note that some Eskies may also have food allergies. While some American Eskimo dogs have a robust digestive system and can consume a diverse range of foods without experiencing any side effects, others have a more sensitive constitution and may not be able to tolerate certain types of food.

Regular veterinary check-ups can help detect any health issues early on and prevent them from becoming more severe. Make sure to schedule regular check-ups with your vet to ensure your American Eskimo is healthy and happy.

grooming tips

Eskies have double coats which are dense and white. and their coats are used to keeping out the cold. even though they don't require frequently grooming, brushing your lovely Eskies daily could prevent matting and tangling.

Trimming their nails regularly could prevent painful splitting, cracking, or a broken nail.

Clean your Ekies' ears regularly to remove any excess wax or debris.

Brush your American Eskimo's teeth regularly to prevent tartar buildup and maintain good oral health.

exercise tips

Eskies need at least an hour of exercise per day. they are playful and energetic, so outdoor activities are important to them.

Your Eskies when exercising outdoors, as they may have a tendency to chase small animals like squirrels or rabbits.

Avoid over-exercising your American Eskimo, especially when they are young or elderly. It's important to gradually increase the intensity and duration of exercise to avoid injury or exhaustion.

Every American Eskimo is unique and may have different exercise needs based on their age, health, and personality. Consult with your veterinarian to develop an exercise plan that is tailored to your dog's individual needs.

nutrition Tips

Some Eskies are prone to overeating and are highly food motivated. Therefore, watch your dog's calorie consumption and weight level.

Choose high-quality dog food, according to your Eskies' age, size, and activity level.

Avoid feeding your Eskies table scraps or human food

Feed your Eskies on a regular schedule to prevent overeating and digestive issues.
